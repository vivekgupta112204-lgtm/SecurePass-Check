Are Online Password Checkers Safe? The Honest Truth

By SecurePass Team • 6 min read

It is the ultimate irony: typing your secret password into a website to check if it's safe. Is it a trap? Is it logging your data? Here is how to tell the good tools from the bad ones.

The Two Types of Checkers

Not all password tools are created equal. They generally fall into two categories:

1. Server-Side Checkers (Dangerous)

In this model, when you type your password and hit "Check," the website sends that text string across the internet to their database server. The server analyzes it and sends back the result.

The Risk: Even if they use SSL (HTTPS), you are entrusting that specific website administrator not to log your password. If their server is hacked, or if they are malicious, your password is compromised.

2. Client-Side Checkers (Safe - Like Us)

In this model, the website sends a small piece of code (JavaScript) to your browser once when the page loads. When you type your password, the calculation happens on your own computer's processor.

The Benefit: The password never leaves your device. You could literally pull the ethernet cable out of your computer, type your password, and the tool would still work.

How to Verify SecurePass Check

We believe in "Trust, but Verify." Here is how you can prove our tool is safe:

  1. Load the page: Go to our home page.
  2. Disconnect the Internet: Turn off Wi-Fi or unplug your cable.
  3. Test it: Type a password. You will see the strength meter still works instantly.

This proves that no data is being sent to a remote server. If we needed to send data to check it, the tool would fail when offline.

Best Practices When Using Checkers

Even with safe tools, we recommend:

  • Never type your actual banking password into any checker.
  • Use similar variations to test the logic (e.g., if your password is Apple@123, test Orange#456 to see the strength).
  • Check the privacy policy (like ours) to ensure no logging occurs.
