5 Common Password Mistakes That Lead to Hacks

By SecurePass Team • 8 min read

Hackers don't usually "hack" passwords in the way you see in movies (typing furiously at a green screen). They exploit human laziness and predictability. Here are the top 5 mistakes that open the door to them.

1. Password Reuse (The Cardinal Sin)

This is the #1 cause of major breaches. You use the same password for your Netflix account as you do for your bank.

The Scenario: A low-security forum you joined 5 years ago gets hacked. Your email and password are leaked to the Dark Web. Hackers then use "Credential Stuffing" bots to try that email/password combination on Amazon, PayPal, Gmail, and thousands of other sites. If you reused the password, they are in.

2. Using Personal Information

Social Engineering is the art of manipulating people into giving up confidential info. But often, you give it up voluntarily on social media.

  • Your dog's name? It's on your Instagram.
  • Your birth year? It's on Facebook.
  • Your favorite team? It's in your Twitter bio.

Hackers use "wordlists" tailored to you. If your password is Rover1995, it will be guessed in seconds.

3. Pattern usage (Keyboard Walks)

Passwords like qwerty, 123456, or qazwsx (walking down the left side of the keyboard) are the first strings checked by brute-force software.

4. Thinking "Substitutions" Are Clever

Replacing 'a' with '@' or 'i' with '1' is called "Leet Speak." It was clever in 1990. Today, every cracking rule-set accounts for these substitutions automatically. P@$$w0rd is statistically just as weak as password to a computer.

5. Ignoring Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)

Even the strongest password can be phished. 2FA acts as a safety net. If you don't have 2FA enabled, your password is your single point of failure. Always enable SMS or App-based (Google Authenticator) 2FA.
